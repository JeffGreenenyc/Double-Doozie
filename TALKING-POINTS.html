🌟 Interview Talking Points — How to Explain This Project
What problem were you solving?

Businesses lose 20–30% of customers every year without knowing which ones or why. I wanted to build a system that flags customers likely to leave before they actually do — early enough to do something about it.

What data did you use?

The UCI Online Retail dataset — 541,000 real transactions from a UK retailer over one year. I used purchase history to calculate how recently, how often, and how much each customer spent.

What did you find?

49% of customers were at high churn risk, and they represented £924K in recoverable revenue. The top 22% of customers (Champions) drove 64.6% of all revenue — yet they were getting the same emails as people who hadn't bought in 6 months.

What would you recommend?

Stop sending everyone the same message. Champions need VIP treatment, not discount codes. At-Risk customers need a personal win-back offer. Lost customers need one last-chance email — then remove them from your list. Each segment gets its own playbook.

What technique did you use?

I used RFM scoring (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) to segment customers, then built a Random Forest machine learning model to predict who would churn. The model achieved an AUC of 0.77, meaning it correctly identifies at-risk customers 77% of the time.

Why does it matter to the business?

Every percentage point of churn you prevent is pure recovered revenue. If 15% of at-risk customers respond to a re-engagement campaign at an average order of £480, that's £139K without acquiring a single new customer. Retention is almost always cheaper than acquisition.

What was the hardest part?

Avoiding data leakage. My first model had a perfect AUC of 1.0 — which sounds great but is actually a warning sign. The churn label and the Recency feature were measuring the same thing. I fixed it by training on months 1–10 and labeling churn on months 11–12.

What would you do with real company data?

Connect it to live CRM data (Salesforce, HubSpot) so the model re-scores customers every month automatically. Set up triggered email workflows so at-risk customers get a win-back sequence without anyone manually pulling a list.
