Overview

I built a single source of truth that ties marketing spend to revenue outcomes with consistent channel taxonomy, time windows, and documented attribution rules. I layered cohort quality on top so budget decisions account for acquisition quality—not just short‑term ROAS.

Bottom line
If we do nothing, the business keeps arguing about the numbers. If we do this, the decision becomes measurable and repeatable.
The problem

Spend, traffic, and conversions lived in different places with inconsistent definitions. That turned reporting into debates and budget moves into guesswork.

What I built

A governed channel taxonomy (UTM/source mapping), a simple explainable attribution heuristic (last non‑direct within 7 days), and a KPI mart consumed by Power BI for weekly ops + monthly strategy.

Key insights

Efficiency and cohort quality don’t always align. Some channels look efficient short‑term but acquire lower‑retention customers; cohort revenue per acquired customer belongs in budget conversations.

Recommendation

Rebalance using a two‑factor score (efficiency + cohort quality), move spend in small steps (10–15%), and use a stop‑loss rule + weekly measurement to avoid whiplash.

Next iteration

Add incrementality tests (geo holdouts/platform lift) and response curves to model diminishing returns more precisely.
